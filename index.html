What this site does

This tool estimates electricity use and monthly cost for common household appliances in Australia. Enter an appliance wattage, how long you use it, and a tariff (price per kWh) to see the impact.

Australia context (placeholder):
  • Electricity is billed per kilowatt-hour (kWh).
  • Tariffs vary by state, retailer and usage tier; use your own bill’s rate for accuracy.
  • Energy Rating Labels (★ to ★★★★★) help compare appliance efficiency.

Television Energy Use in Australia

Modern TVs vary in power draw. LED/LCD models tend to be lower than similarly sized OLEDs. Actual consumption depends on screen size, brightness, HDR, gaming mode, and standby settings.

TypeTypical range (W)Notes
LED/LCD (32–55")50–120 WLower draw at reduced brightness
OLED (48–77")90–200 WHigher with HDR content
Standby (any TV)0.3–2 WTurn off at the wall to avoid trickle use
How to estimate cost: kWh/day = (Watts ÷ 1000) × hours used. Monthly cost ≈ kWh/day × days × tariff.
